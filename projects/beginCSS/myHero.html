My Hero: Manny "Pacman" Pacquiao

Emmanuel Dapidran Pacquiao is former professional boxer from the Philippines. He has been referred to multiple times as "The Fighting Pride of The Philippines" in respect to his achievements in the sport. Manny is the first and only eight-division world champion, having won a world title in eight different divisions. He is also the first to win a world title in four of the eight original boxing weight classes. He was active in the sport for a long time that he was able to become the first and only four decade world champion, having won world titles in the 1990's, 2000's, 2010's, and 2020's. Manny has many more accolades in the sport and he is my hero because of his origin. Coming from a life so deprived of resources that he had to fight every Sunday to earn an equivalent of $2 just so he can afford some medicine for his mom has taught me that my situtaion is almost never an excuse to not grow in life. Yes, Manny is in the 99.99% percentile in terms achievements and origin difference, but his perserverance has showed that results are almost always expected when we strive for our goals.

Flyweight (112 lbs) World Champion
Knocking out Chatchai Sasakul in the 8th-round to win the WBC Flywieght Title. (1998)
Super Bantamweight (122 lbs) World Champion
Taking a fight on short notice to stop Lehlo Ledwaba on the 6th-round by technical knockout (TKO) to claim the IBF Super BantamWeight Title. (2001)
Featherweight (126 lbs) World Champion
Dominated the dominant Marco Antonio Barrera and won by TKO in the 11th-round to lineally claim The Ring Featherweight Title. (2003)
Super Featherweight (130 lbs) World Champion
Narrowly winning a split decision against Juan Manuel Marquez in their second bout to capture the WBC Super Featherweight Title. (2008)
Lightweight (135 lbs) World Champion
Moving up a weight class to TKO the champion, David Diaz, in the 9th-round to win the WBC Lightweight Title. (2008)
Light Welterwight (140 lbs) World Champion
Stopped Ricky Hatton in the 2nd-round with the most spactacular knockout of his career and won The Ring Light Welterwight Title. (2009)
Welterwight (147 lbs) World Champion
In the most testing fight of his career, Pacquiao TKOd the hall of famer Miguel Cotto in the 12th-round for the WBO Welterwight Title. (2009)
Super Welterwight (154 lbs) World Champion
Being outweighed by 17 lbs, Manny still dominated Antonio Margarito to get a unanimous decision win for the WBC Super Welterwight Title. (2010)
